From Trash to Tech: How Startups Are Turning
Waste into Smart Devices






Every year, the world generates over 50 million tons of electronic waste—most
of it ending up in landfills. But a new wave of startups is transforming this trash into cutting-edge tech.


These companies are rethinking how devices are made by using recycled plastics,
metals, and components to build smart gadgets like sensors, wearables, and even modular smartphones. It’s all part
of a growing movement toward the circular economy, where waste is reused, not discarded.


Examples include:

Circular Devices with its modular PuzzlePhone.

Precious Plastic, enabling communities to create tools and devices from plastic waste.

using recycled sensors for solar energy monitoring.


The benefits go beyond sustainability:

Cheaper, accessible tech for underserved regions.

Reduced carbon footprint.

New green jobs in recycling and innovation.
